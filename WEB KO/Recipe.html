Beef Stroganoff

It is a luxurious dish of tender strips of beef, sautéed in a rich and creamy sauce made with cream, mushrooms, and onions. The flavors of the beef, mushrooms, and onions are perfectly balanced, and the sour cream (substituted with all purpose cream) gives it a tangy and rich finish. Imagine taking a forkful of the beef and mushrooms, with a creamy and savory sauce, and letting the flavors melt in your mouth. The dish is often served over a bed of egg noodles, which soak up the delicious sauce and make each bite even more delectable. I like mine served with mashed potatoes on the side or just plain, perfectly cooked white rice. This dish is ideal for the "bed weather" and will undoubtedly warm you from the inside out.
Here is my recipe for you to try at home:
Ingredients:
* 1/2 kg beef tenderloin or sirloin steak
* Salt and pepper to taste
* 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
* 2 tablespoons butter
* 1 onion, finely chopped
* Canned mushrooms, sliced
* 4 cloves of garlic, minced
* 2 cups beef stock
* 1 cup all-purpose cream
Instructions:
1 Cut the beef into thin strips and season with salt and pepper.
2 Dredge the beef in flour. In a large skillet, heat the butter over medium-high heat.
3 Add the beef and sauté for 2-3 minutes, or until browned.
4 Add the onion, mushrooms, and garlic and sauté for 2-3 minutes, or until softened.
5 Stir in the beef broth and bring it to a simmer.
6 Cook just enough until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened.
7 Remove from the heat and stir in the all-purpose cream.
8 Serve the beef stroganoff, garnish with fresh parsley or chives if desired. I also added some crispy garlic on top.

Beef Tapa Recipe

My recipe for Beef tapa is a mouth-watering dish that will transport your taste buds straight to Calatagan, Batangas (where my roots reside). This thinly sliced beef is marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, garlic, and spices, then pan-fried to perfection for a smoky, savory flavor that will make your taste buds sing.
To make this dish, you'll need:
* 1 lb thinly sliced beef (sirloin or flank steak work well)
* 1/4 cup soy sauce
* 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
* 1/4 cup brown sugar
* 1 head of garlic, minced
* 1 tsp ground black pepper
* 1/2 tsp paprika
Instructions:
1 To prepare the beef tapa, begin by combining soy sauce, calamansi juice, brown sugar, garlic, black pepper, salt, and paprika in a large bowl.
2 Add the beef to the marinade, ensuring each piece is completely covered. Seal the bowl with plastic wrap and place it in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results.
3 Next, heat the pan to a high temperature. Pan-fry the beef for 2-3 minutes per side or until it reaches your desired level of doneness.
4 Serve the beef tapa with steamed white rice, and for added texture, consider serving it with some stir-fried vegetables. Garnish with some sesame seeds.
5 Each bite of this dish will offer a bold and savory flavor, a smoky char from the maillard, and the tenderness of the beef.

Beef Nilaga Recipe

Beef nilaga, also known as "beef stew" in English, is a traditional Filipino dish that is a true comfort food. Imagine tender chunks of beef that have been slow-cooked to perfection in a comforting broth made with onions, potatoes, napa cabbage, corn, and more leafy vegetables. The beef is so tender that it practically melts in your mouth, while the vegetables add a nice crunch and sweetness to the dish.
To make this delicious dish, you will need the following ingredients:
* 750 grams of beef chuck or beef shank, which should be cut into 2-inch cubes.
* 2 medium-sized onions that have been chopped
* 4 cloves of minced garlic.
* 5 cups of beef broth
* 2 cups of water
* 2 potatoes that have been peeled and cut into cubes
* 1 medium-sized cabbage cut into wedges
* 1/2 napa cabbage cut into wedges
* 2 pieces of corn cut into pieces
* 1 bunch of Bok Choy trimmed of its root,
* 1 bunch of green beans that have been trimmed and cut in half.
* Season the dish with fish sauce and whole peppercorns to taste.
Instructions
1 To prepare the dish, heat some oil in a large pot over medium heat.
2 Add the onions and garlic and sauté until fragrant.
3 Add the beef and cook until browned on all sides.
4 Pour in the beef broth, water, and whole peppercorns, and bring to a boil.
5 Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 2 hours or until the beef is fork-tender.
6 Add the potatoes and continue to simmer for another 15-20 minutes, then add the remaining vegetables and simmer for another 3-5 minutes, or until the vegetables are cooked to desired texture.
7 Season to taste with fish sauce.
8 Serve hot and enjoy with perfectly cooked steamed rice.

Crispy Pork Sisig Recipe

Crispy Pork Sisig is a delectable dish that will awaken your taste buds with its savory and tangy flavors. The dish is prepared by first frying chunks of pork belly until they are crispy and golden brown on the outside. The pork is then chopped into small pieces and mixed with a combination of diced onions, red and green chili peppers and a savory tangy citrus marinade. The mixture is then cooked until the flavors meld together, infusing the pork with a mouth-watering smoky taste.
To make Crispy Pork Sisig, you will need the following ingredients:
* 1/2 kg Pork belly, cut into cubes
* 1 onion, diced
* 2-3 red chili peppers, diced
* 2-3 green chili peppers, chopped slant.
* 1/4 cup soy sauce
* 1/4 cup lime or calamansi juice
* 2 cloves of garlic, minced
* Salt and pepper, to taste
* Mayonnaise (I used korean brand mayo for this)
Instructions:
1 In a pan, fry the pre-cooked pork belly cubes until golden brown and very crispy. Drain the pork on paper towels to remove excess oil.
2 In the same pan, sauté the onions and chili peppers until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
3 Add the fried pork to the pan, along with the soy sauce, lime juice, salt, and pepper. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld together.
4 Serve the Crispy Pork Sisig hot, garnished with chopped green and red chilies, onions, slices of lemon, crispy garlic, and drizzled with good mayonnaise. Enjoy with a cold beer or a steaming bowl of rice.

Crispy Chicken Ala King Recipe

Pinoy Crispy Chicken Ala King is an irresistible dish that is sure to make your taste buds go crazy with its rich and savory flavors. The dish is made by first coating chicken cutlets in a mixture of flour, salt, pepper, and egg wash, then deep-frying them until golden and crispy. The chicken is then paired with a savory, creamy sauce made with chicken broth, milk, and butter, as well as a touch of corn starch to thicken.The dish is then served over a bed of steaming rice, making a delicious, hearty, and satisfying meal.
Here's how to make Pinoy Crispy Chicken ala king:
Ingredients:
1/2 kg Chicken breast, thinly sliced
1 cup all-purpose flour
2 eggs, beaten
1 cup breadcrumbs
1 cup frozen mixed vegetables
1/2 cup diced bell peppers
1/2 cup diced onion
1/2 cup chicken broth
1/2 cup milk
2 tbsp butter
2 tbsp corn starch
4 cloves of garlic, minced
Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
1 Prepare the thinly sliced chicken breasts by seasoning them with salt and pepper, then coating them in a mixture of flour, beaten eggs and breadcrumbs.
2 In a pan, heat oil over medium-high heat and fry the chicken until golden brown and crispy. Drain the excess oil using paper towels.
3 In the same pan, sauté garlic, bell peppers, onion, and mixed vegetables until they are softened.
4 In a separate bowl, combine chicken broth, milk, corn starch, and butter and mix well. Pour the mixture into the pan and stir.
5 Simmer the mixture and continue cooking until the sauce thickens.
6 Serve the sautéed vegetables in a creamy white sauce over the crispy chicken. Enjoy this delicacy with steaming rice.

Crispy Calamari (Pinoy Calamares) Recipe

Crispy-fried Calamari is a dish that is sure to delight your taste buds with its perfectly golden exterior and tender, succulent interior. The key to achieving the perfect crispiness is to dust the calamari rings in a mixture of flour, cornstarch, and spices before deep-frying them to perfection.
Ingredients:
1/2 kg of cleaned squid
1 cup of all-purpose flour
1 cup of cornstarch
1 tsp paprika
1 tsp garlic powder
1 tsp Onion powder
1 tbsp of sugar
Salt and pepper to taste
Oil for deep-frying
Instructions:
1 Cut the squid into slices of approximately 1/2 inch in thickness.
2 In a mixing bowl, blend together the flour, cornstarch, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until evenly distributed.
3 Heat oil in a fryer or in a deep frying pan.
4 Place the squid slices in the flour mixture, making sure they are fully coated.
5 Gently place the coated squid slices in the hot oil and fry for 2-3 minutes or until golden brown and crispy. Be careful not to overcook, as it can cause the squid to become rubbery in texture.
6 Remove the squid from the oil and place on a paper towel to drain.
7 Serve immediately with a dipping sauce of your choice. I personally prefer using my homemade spiced vinegar in this recipe.

"Ginataang Alimasag sa Kalabasa at Sitaw" Recipe

"Ginataang Alimasag sa Kalabasa at Sitaw" is a flavorful and satisfying dish that brings tropical warmth with its rich and creamy coconut milk base, and the delicately balanced flavors of crab, kalabasa, and sitaw. This recipe is the epitome of comfort food, providing both delicious taste and nutritious benefits.
Ingredients:
1 lb of crab meat
2 cups of coconut milk
1/2 cup of sliced kalabasa
1/2 cup of sliced sitaw
3 cloves of minced garlic
1 onion, chopped
1 thumb of sliced ginger
2 pieces green chilies
1/4 cup of fish sauce
Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
1 In a pot, sauté garlic, ginger and onions until translucent.
2 Add in the fish sauce, kalabasa and sitaw. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
3 Add in the crab meat and continue to cook for 2-3 minutes.
4 Pour in the coconut milk and bring it to a boil.
5 Reduce the heat to low, add green chilies, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and the sauce has thickened.
6 Season with salt and pepper to taste.
7 Serve hot with perfectly-cooked steamed rice.

Pork and Shrimp Siomai Recipe

Pork and Shrimp Siomai is a savory Asian-inspired dumpling that will arouse your taste buds. Consider plump, juicy shrimp paired with tender pork, all wrapped in a delicate wonton wrapper. The dumplings are perfectly steamed, bringing a mouthwatering aroma that will make your stomach growl with anticipation. As the savory pork and shrimp combine with the aromatics, each bite is a symphony of flavors.
To make your own Pork and Shrimp Siomai, you will need:
1/2 kg ground pork
1/2 kg shrimp, peeled and deveined
1/4 cup green onions, finely chopped
1/4 cup parsley, finely chopped
1 head of garlic, minced
1 onion
1 thumb of ginger, grated
1/4 cup oyster sauce tbsp soy sauce
2 tbsps sesame oil
3 whole eggs
Salt and pepper to taste
Wonton wrappers
Instructions:
1 In a large mixing bowl, combine ground pork, shrimp, green onions, parsley, garlic, onion, ginger, oyster sauce, sesame oil, eggs, and pepper. Mix well.
2 Take one wonton wrapper and place a teaspoon of the mixture in the center.
3 Gently gather the edges of the wrapper together, forming a dumpling shape.
4 Repeat steps until all the mixture is used up.
5 Place the dumplings in a steamer and steam for 15-25 minutes or until cooked through.
6 Serve with dipping sauce made with chili garlic oil, soy sauce, and calamansi. Enjoy your homemade Pork and Shrimp Siomai while it's hot.

Bibimbap

is a Korean dish that is a true feast for the senses. Imagine a warm bowl of steaming rice, topped with a colorful array of vegetables, all nestled alongside a perfectly cooked sunny-side up egg. The dish is finished with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and seaweed, creating a symphony of flavors in every bite. The texture of the crispy vegetables paired with the softness of the rice and the runny egg yolk is a true culinary experience.
To make your own Bibimbap, you will need:
1 1/2 cups of cooked white rice
1/4 cup of sliced carrots
1/4 cup of sliced cabbage
1/2 cup of bean sprouts
1 egg
3 tbsps of Gochujang (Korean chili paste)
2 tbsps of sesame oil
2 tbsps of sesame seeds
1 sheet of dried seaweed
1/4 cup sliced to strips pork
1 tbsp sugar
Green onions
Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
1 In a pan, heat sesame oil and sauté the sliced carrots, cabbage, spring onions, and bean sprouts (make it in separate batches for presentation).
2 Season with salt and pepper.
3 In a separate pan, fry the egg to your preference and then set it aside
4 Pan-fry your pork in sesame oil, add 2 tablespoons of the chili paste, sugar, season with salt and pepper.
5 In a bowl, place a cup of cooked rice and top it with fried egg.
6 Put the stir-fried vegetables around the rice.
7 Sprinkle it with crushed seaweed and toasted sesame seeds, then serve.

Samgye-tang Recipe

Samgye-tang is a traditional Korean dish that will make your taste buds dance with delight. Imagine a hot and comforting bowl of chicken soup, infused with the flavors of ginseng, garlic, and rice. The tender chunks of chicken are cooked to perfection, falling off the bone and melts in your mouth. The broth is rich and savory, with a hint of congee vibe from the rice. As you take a spoonful of broth, you'll feel the warmth spread through your body, making you feel nourished and satisfied.
This dish is not only delicious but also believed to have medicinal properties.
To make your own Samgye-tang, you will need:
1 whole young chicken
1 cup of rice (I used glutinous rice in this recipe)
1 onion, sliced
2 cups Ginseng-infused broth (available in some Korean stores)
1 head of garlic, peeled
4 cups of water
1/2 cup of black fungus (Tengang daga - Optional, used in this recipe for flavor and texture)
Chopped scallions
Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
1 Rinse the chicken and place it in a pot with water, ginseng-infused broth, onion, and garlic. Bring it to a boil and then reduce the heat to low. Simmer for about 1-2 hours or until the chicken is fully cooked.
2 Remove the chicken from the pot, set aside and let it cool.
3 Add the glutinous rice and black fungus to the pot and simmer for another 20-30 minutes or until the rice is cooked.
4 Place the chicken meat back into the pot and season with salt and pepper.
5 Serve in a bowl, top it with chopped scallions, and enjoy your homemade Samgye-tang!
(The eggs here are my home-made Mayak Gyeran - A Korean marinated eggs, perfectly cooked to make it jammy)

Sweet and Spicy buttered Mud Crabs and Tiger Prawns & Aligue Fried Rice Recipe

Indulge in the flavors and textures of succulent mud crab, infused with a bold and spicy butter sauce. The sweetness of the crab is perfectly balanced by the heat of the spices, creating a symphony of flavors on your tongue. The tiger prawns, plump and juicy, complement the boldness of the crab and the sauce. Fresh corn adds a subtle sweetness and satisfying crunch to each bite.
As you savor each mouthful, the tangy and juicy lemon wedges cut through the richness of the butter sauce, leaving a refreshing aftertaste. On the side, a bed of Aligue fried rice awaits, each grain infused with the rich and buttery flavor of crab fat, adding an extra layer of decadence to the dish.
To make your own sweet and spicy buttered seafood dish, you will need:
1 kg mud crabs, cleaned
1/2 kg tiger prawns
2 pcs corn, cut into pieces
1 1/2 cups of unsalted butter
2 heads of garlic, minced
5-10 pcs or red chilis (according to preference)
2 1/2 tbsps of sugar
1 tsp paprika
1 tsp Atsuete powder
Salt and pepper to taste
1 lemon, cut into wedges
For the Aligue fried rice:
3-4 cups of cooked white rice
1/2 cup of crab fat or butter
3 cloves of garlic, minced
1 onion, diced
2 tbsp of chopped red bell peppers
1/2 cup green peas
Salt and pepper to taste
Instruction
1 Boil the mud crabs for 12 minutes, then add the tiger prawns and cook for another 5 minutes before setting aside.
2 When the mud crabs have cooled, cut them in half so that the flavors can infuse with the crab meat later.
3 In a pan, melt butter and sauté garlic, red chilies, sugar, atsuete powder, and paprika.
4 Add the mud crabs and tiger prawns and stir until they are fully coated and infused with flavors.
5 Add pre-cooked corn, salt, and pepper. Cook for another 3–5 minutes.
6 For the Aligue fried rice, in a pan, heat crab fat or butter and sauté garlic, onions, red bell peppers, and green peas. Add the cooked rice and stir until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.
7 Serve the seafood dish on a plate, topped with lemon wedges. Serve the Aligue fried rice on the side.

Sweet and Spicy Orange "Fall-of-the-bone" Ribs Recipe

Introducing my culinary masterpiece that will tempt your taste buds and leave you wanting more. Imagine succulent, fall-off-the-bone ribs that have been slow-cooked to perfection, infused with a blend of spices and the natural sweetness of real oranges. The ribs are then finished with a sprinkle of sesame seeds, adding a delightful crunch and nuttiness to every bite. Served alongside the dish are fresh wedges of lemon and orange, adding a burst of acidic citrus to balance out the flavors. This dish is a true celebration of flavor and texture, satisfying your cravings for something sweet, spicy, and oh-so-tender. It's the perfect meal to share with family and friends, and will be sure to leave a lasting impression on your taste buds. Don't wait, come and try it today! Sweet and Spicy Orange Ribs Recipe
Ingredients:
2 slabs of pork ribs
1/4 cup honey
1/4 cup soy sauce
2 cloves of garlic, minced
1 tbsp grated ginger
2 tbsp brown sugar
1 teaspoon chili flakes
1 tbsp of smoke paprika
1 1/2 tablespoons of sesame seeds
1 lemon, cut into wedges
5-6 pcs large oranges
Instructions:
1 Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
2 Combine the orange juice, half a lemon juice, honey, soy sauce, garlic, ginger, smoked paprika, and chili flakes in a bowl.
3 Place the bite-size ribs in a baking dish and toss to coat with the marinade. Make sure the ribs are completely covered in the marinade.
4 Place the baking dish in the oven and cover with foil. Bake the ribs for 2 1/2 hours at 350°F.
5 Remove the foil and brush the remaining marinade over the ribs.
6 Return the ribs to the oven and bake for another 30 minutes, then sprinkle with sesame seeds and continue to cook for another 30 minutes.
7 Remove the ribs from the oven and set aside for 10-15 minutes to rest.
8 Arrange the ribs on a platter with the lemon and orange wedges on the side. Enjoy!
